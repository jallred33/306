# Malabou, from *What Should We Do with Our Brain?*

1. Common sense tells us that the brain is a biological given, that it is roughly the same for all humans, and that human brains have been the same since the emergence of humans as a species about 300,000 ago. What does Malabou do to trouble that set of assumptions at the top of the chapter?
2. On p. 4, Malabou write, "Our brain in plastic, and we do now know it." What are some of the meanings that Malabou attributes to this "plasticity"? What are the obstacles to our "knowing" this "plasticity"?
3. How does Malabou's preferred term, "plasticity," relate to the term "flexibility" that one finds so often in discussions of late capitalist labor markets? What are the similarities and differences between these two terms? What are the implications of rethinking of the prototypically "flexible" worker (say, a "gig" worker or a worker in a "precarious," short-term contract) as the posessor of a "plastic" brain?
